Water Challenges in Kenya

Understanding rainfall, water sources, and the impact on communities

Rainfall & Water Sources

Kenya experiences highly variable rainfall across different regions. The country has two main rainy seasons: the Long Rains (March-May) and Short Rains (October-December). Average rainfall varies significantly from 250 mm/year in arid areas to over 2,000 mm/year in highland regions.

Main water sources include rivers, lakes, dams, and underground aquifers. However, these are unevenly distributed, often leaving arid and semi-arid regions with scarce access.

Issues Caused by Rainfall Variability

Irregular rainfall patterns lead to:

  • Droughts affecting over 3 million people annually in northern and eastern Kenya.
  • Flooding during heavy rains, causing displacement and destruction of infrastructure.
  • Reduced agricultural productivity, threatening food security.
  • Contamination of water sources, increasing the risk of waterborne diseases.
